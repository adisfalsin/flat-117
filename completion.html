The Transformation

Before & After

A side-by-side record of every space reimagined — from dated and functional to refined and contemporary.

Bathroom

✕  Before

Original bathroom from the entrance: dated floor tiles in a busy diagonal pattern, pedestal basin, and standard bathtub with no design cohesion.

Full room view revealing the compact layout. Basic beige wall tiles, exposed cistern toilet, and a standalone shower screen with no contemporary detail.

Serviceable but uninspiring: a chrome towel rail, bare walls above the tile line, and a macerator toilet unit cluttering the floor space.

Close-up of the original pedestal basin — standard issue with a single lever tap and no vanity storage whatsoever.

✓  After

Transformed entrance view: large-format Regal Grey Matt tiles floor to ceiling, a floating vanity unit with open shelving, and recessed LED downlights creating a spa-like ambience.

The complete picture: a rainfall shower over the repositioned bath, frameless glass screen, concealed-cistern WC unit with clean line boxing, and a Vent-A-Matic extractor discreetly integrated above.

The vessel basin with Hansgrohe tall monobloc tap — a polished statement piece in chrome atop a White Sparkle quartz worktop. A heated ladder towel rail completes the luxury finish.

The backlit oval LED mirror illuminates the vessel basin vanity — two open shelves below in quartz-faced board, fully integrated into the tiled alcove. Hotel-grade finish throughout.

Kitchen

✕  Before

The original kitchen-living space: cream slab-front units with black splashback, an exposed washing machine, and ageing electric hob — functional but far from premium.

Second angle confirming the dated palette: worn chrome handles, dark countertops, and an undistinguished layout offering no personality or spatial flow.

✓  After

The new Howdens Clerkenwell White Gloss kitchen: handleless high-gloss units, White Sparkle quartz worktops, an induction hob, integrated stainless-steel extractor, and a built-in microwave oven — all set against a seamless glass splashback.

Wider view showing the full L-shaped layout with peninsular breakfast bar, a modern black undermount sink with chrome mixer tap, and the new engineered oak-effect floor running through into the living area — the entire room feels twice as large.
