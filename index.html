Scope Change Notice — Client Review Required

Following inspection of the existing bulkhead and kitchen zone, a number of scope adjustments are required. The bulkhead conceals unfinished structure, open cabling and an incomplete ceiling void that cannot be fully remediated within the agreed programme. This document outlines the observations, photographic evidence, and recommended changes to the specification.

Progress Observations

Bulkhead — Cannot Be RemovedInternal inspection reveals unfinished ceiling void, open cabling and incomplete structure. Full removal is not viable within the agreed programme.

Bulkhead — Inspection Findings

Bulkhead Void — Internal Condition

Action Required
🔌
Exposed & Unprotected Cabling: The bulkhead void contains live cabling that is hung loose and unprotected. This cannot be concealed, re-routed or made safe within the current programme without significant additional works.
🔲
Unfinished Ceiling & Walls Inside Void: The interior of the bulkhead shows raw, unplastered surfaces on both the ceiling and walls. Removing the bulkhead casing would expose these areas, requiring full plastering remediation before any finish work could proceed.
⏱
Programme Impact: Full removal, re-wire, re-plastering of the ceiling void and walls is not achievable within the timeframe we have established with the client. Proceeding would extend the programme significantly and introduce risk to adjoining areas.
🏛
Cooker Hood — Chimney Style Not Viable: Due to the bulkhead remaining in situ, a chimney-style hood as originally desired cannot be fitted. The recommended alternative is a built-in integrated cooker hood, which will work within the existing bulkhead constraints and deliver a clean, flush finish to the kitchen.

Summary of Impact & Client Actions Required
  • Bulkhead to remain in its current position — full removal is not within the agreed programme scope or timeline.
  • Chimney-style cooker hood is not achievable with the bulkhead in place. A built-in integrated hood is the recommended alternative and will be fitted accordingly.
  • Wall units to be revised from 900mm (original) to 720mm height — this still represents a significant improvement over the existing 600mm units in situ.
  • Client confirmation is required on the 720mm wall unit specification before order can be placed.
  • Any decision to proceed with bulkhead removal would require a separate scope, programme extension and cost review.
